How the Calculator Works

Our crypto tax calculator is a client-side application that performs all computations directly in your web browser using JavaScript. When you enter your trade details -- purchase price, sale price, quantity, holding period, cost basis method, and tax bracket -- the calculator estimates your capital gain or loss and applies the appropriate federal tax rate.

For short-term holdings (less than one year), your ordinary income tax rate is applied. For long-term holdings (one year or more), the preferential long-term capital gains rates of 0%, 15%, or 20% are used based on your tax bracket. The calculator supports three cost basis methods:

  • FIFO (First In, First Out): The default IRS method. Assumes your earliest-purchased units are sold first.
  • LIFO (Last In, First Out): Assumes your most recently acquired units are sold first. Often results in smaller gains in a rising market.
  • Specific Identification: Allows you to choose exactly which units to sell, giving you the most control over your tax outcome.

Privacy commitment: Your financial data never leaves your device. We have no server-side processing, no databases storing trade information, and no tracking of calculator inputs. Your privacy is not a feature -- it is our foundation.

Why Crypto Tax Education Matters

The IRS treats cryptocurrency as property, which means every sale, trade, or spending event can trigger a taxable event. Since 2025, the wash sale rule now applies to digital assets, and beginning with the 2026 tax year, exchanges are required to issue Form 1099-DA to both taxpayers and the IRS. The era of unreported crypto activity is over.

We publish detailed, well-researched guides to help you understand the rules and make informed decisions. Our content covers topics including:

  • How the IRS classifies and taxes cryptocurrency
  • The difference between short-term and long-term capital gains rates
  • How to choose the right cost basis method for your situation
  • Tax implications of DeFi activities like staking, yield farming, and liquidity pools
  • Tax loss harvesting strategies and the 2026 wash sale rules
  • Required tax forms: Form 8949, Schedule D, Form 1099-DA
  • Record-keeping best practices
